This is my application to Founders and Coders, Spring 2021

I'm applying for Founders and Coders because I'm serious about pursuing a career in web development. I'm really excited about the idea of collaborative, peer-led learning and I feel it's an environment that I will thrive in.

A little about me: I'm a 27 year old Finance & Business graduate from the University of Sussex. I grew up on a council estate in South East London, and I owe everything to my hard-working parents. Despite growing up on low income with government benefits, my parents always made sure that my sister and I had what we needed to succeed in our work and studies.

My latest/current role is as Head of Marketing @ Cornerstone.co.uk, a D2C subscription-based startup based in London. As a small company of less than 10 people, I've had the opportunity to learn a huge amount around all things tech and business in only a few years.

I joined Cornerstone as a Customer Support agent, with 0 development and marketing experience. Through great opportunity, consistent effort and dedication, today I've become a successful, technical marketer.

I've made countless marketing landing pages and emails using HTML & CSS. I work closely with our in-house developers (working with Agile, overseeing sprints & creating comprehensive tickets). I have also made small deployments to our codebase with Github. I've worked with Shopify, Mailchimp, AWS and SQL too. I've had the opportunity to learn a lot of development skills within my marketing role, and I've come to find a passion for it. This has led me to search for ways to move into web development as a career.

When I came across Founders and Coders, I knew it was special - I couldn't miss the incredible opportunity. With some background experience and determination to make this application, I completed all application requirements in around 3 weeks.

I want to prove that it's possible to make a career shift with enough passion and determination to work hard. I also want to join a community of like-minded individuals. I really hope to hear from you!

Thank you, Di
